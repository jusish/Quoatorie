Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela was a South African
anti-apartheid activist and politician who served
as the first president of South Africa from
1994 to 1999. He was the country's first
black head of state and the first elected
in a fully representative
democratic election.

Walter Elias Disney was an American
animator, film producer and entrepreneur.
A pioneer of the American animation industry,
he introduced several developments in
the production of cartoons.

Henry David Thoreau was an American naturalist,
essayist, poet, and philosopher. A leading
transcendentalist, he is best known for his book
Walden, a reflection upon simple living in natural
surroundings, and his essay "Civil Disobedience",
an argument for disobedience to an unjust state.
